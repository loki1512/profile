KSA 3.0

Design Decisions

Static Search Keywords

Built for repetitive retail catalog patterns like PVC fittings and plumbing components, reducing repetitive typing during bulk billing.

Reverse Discount Logic

Designed around real negotiation workflows. Users can enter negotiated totals directly while discounts are automatically computed.

Revenue vs Cashflow

Dashboard differentiates actual revenue from incoming cashflow to better visualize operational liquidity and credit exposure.

Temporal Snapshotting

Historical transactions preserve cost-price state at transaction time, ensuring long-term analytical correctness.

Smart Catalogue Editing

Unified search and edit workflows reduce duplicate entries and improve operational speed.

Future Direction

Village-wise credit analytics and profit-aware inventory intelligence are planned future extensions.

MEDICAL AI

Architectural Decisions

Why EfficientNet-B0?

The model was selected for deployability rather than benchmark dominance, enabling future low-cost edge deployment.

Edge AI Direction

The system was envisioned for Raspberry Pi integrated fundus-camera setups instead of GPU-heavy hospital infrastructure.

Binary + Severity Pipeline

Initial detection determines DR presence first before severity estimation, improving reliability for binary screening.

Constraint-Aware AI

The project prioritized accessible healthcare AI under deployment and hardware constraints.

Research Outcome

Published at IEEE SCES 2024 and received Best Paper Award.

Core Philosophy

AI systems become meaningful only when they remain usable under real-world limitations.

A Common Thread

At first glance, a retail ERP, a medical AI system, a quantum learning platform and an irrigation controller may appear unrelated.

But each project began with the same observation:

people struggle less because systems are complex, and more because systems rarely adapt to how people actually think and work.

KSA reduced friction in retail workflows. CATTRACK reduced retrieval friction in learning. The DR system explored deployable AI under hardware constraints. The irrigation system automated repetitive monitoring decisions.

Different domains. Same pursuit:

building systems that become useful under real-world constraints.
